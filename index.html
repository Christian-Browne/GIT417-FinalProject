A History of Excellence

With 17 NBA championships, the Los Angeles Lakers are one of the most successful franchises in the league's history. Founded in 1947, the Lakers have consistently been a force to be reckoned with. From the days of George Mikan to the legendary Magic Johnson, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar, Shaquille O'Neal, and Kobe Bryant eras, the Lakers have always been a team to watch in the playoffs.

Unforgettable Playoff Moments

  • 1987 Playoffs: "Skyhook to Victory"

    The Lakers faced off against the Boston Celtics in the 1987 NBA Finals, and in Game 4, Kareem Abdul-Jabbar made one of the most iconic shots in NBA history. With just seconds left in the game and the score tied at 106-106, Abdul-Jabbar hit a skyhook over Robert Parish to give the Lakers a 108-106 lead. The Lakers would go on to win the game and eventually the championship, with Abdul-Jabbar named Finals MVP.

  • 2000 Playoffs: "Shaq Dominates the Pacers"

    In the 2000 NBA Finals, the Lakers faced off against the Indiana Pacers. In Game 2, Shaquille O'Neal put on an absolute clinic, scoring 40 points and grabbing 24 rebounds to lead the Lakers to a 111-104 victory. O'Neal's dominance was on full display, as he shot 60% from the field and made 10 of his 16 free throw attempts. The Lakers would go on to win the championship, with O'Neal named Finals MVP.

  • 2010 Playoffs: "Kobe's Game 7 Heroics"

    The Lakers faced off against the Boston Celtics once again in the 2010 NBA Finals, and in Game 7, Kobe Bryant delivered one of the most memorable performances of his career. With the score tied at 64-64 in the fourth quarter, Bryant hit a tough jumper over Ray Allen to give the Lakers the lead for good. Bryant would go on to score 10 more points in the quarter, finishing with 23 points and 15 rebounds to lead the Lakers to their 16th championship.

  • 2020 Playoffs: "LeBron and AD Lead the Lakers"

    In the 2020 NBA Playoffs, the Lakers faced off against the Miami Heat in the NBA Finals. LeBron James and Anthony Davis were dominant throughout the series, with James averaging 29.8 points, 11.8 rebounds, and 8.5 assists per game, and Davis averaging 25.0 points, 10.7 rebounds, and 2.0 blocks per game. In Game 6, James and Davis combined for 57 points, 19 rebounds, and 6 assists to lead the Lakers to a 106-93 victory and their 17th championship in franchise history.
